[[Set|Sets]] and their operations ([[Complement of a set|complement]], [[Union of sets|union]], [[Intersection of sets|intersection]]) follow some [[Algebras|algebraic]] laws.

- The [[Union of sets|union]] and [[Intersection of sets|intersection]] are [[Commutative property|commutative]].
	- $S\cup T=T\cup S$
	- $S\cap T=T\cap S$
- The [[Union of sets|union]] and [[Intersection of sets|intersection]] are [[Associative property|associative]].
	- $S\cup(T\cup U)=(S\cup T)\cup U$
	- $S\cap(T\cap U)=(S\cap T)\cap U$
- The [[Union of sets|union]] [[Distributive property|distributes over]] [[Intersection of sets|intersection]], and vice versa.
	- $S\cap(T\cup U)=(S\cap T)\cup(S\cap U)$
	- $S\cup(T\cap U)=(S\cup T)\cap(S\cup U)$
- The [[Union of sets|union]] and [[Intersection of sets|intersection]] have the [[Empty set|empty set]] and the [[Universal set|universal set]] as their [[Identity element|identity elements]].
	- $S\cup\emptyset = S$
	- $S\cap\Omega=S$
- [[Complement of a set|Complement]] laws
	- $S\cup S'=\Omega$
	- $S\cap S'=\emptyset$
	- $S''=S$
- [[De Morgan's laws]]:
	- $(\bigcup S_n)'=\bigcap (S_n')$
	- $(\bigcap S_n)'=\bigcup (S_n')$
