The **expected value** (Also called **expectation** or **mean**) of a [[Random variable|random variable]] $X$ is the weighted average of all outcomes. 

|                     | [[Discrete random variable\|Discrete]] | [[Continuous random variable\|Continuous]]   |
| :------------------ | -------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Expectation         | $E[X]=\sum_xxp_X(x)$                   | $E[X]=\int_{-\infty}^\infty xf_X(x)dx$       |
| Expected value rule | $E[f(X)]=\sum_xf(x)p_X(x)$             | $E[f(X)]=\int_{-\infty}^\infty f(x)f_X(x)dx$ |


If two [[Random variable|random variables]] are [[Independent discrete random variable|independent]], then $E[XY]=E[X]E[Y]$. In fact, if we take  [[Functions|functions]] $f$ and $g$, we get $E[f(X)g(Y)]=E[f(X)]E[g(Y)]$
